Leadership & Affiliations
Anamprem NGO – COVID-19 Relief Work

Volunteering with Anamprem during the COVID-19 lockdown was one of the most eye-opening and emotional experiences of my life. As migrant labourers were forced to leave cities and walk hundreds of kilometers back to their villages, many were left without food, shelter, or medical aid. I joined the relief efforts to distribute food packets, sanitary pads, and first aid kits to those in need.

What struck me most was the sheer desperation — people were hungry, scared, and exhausted. I remember handing a food packet to a man who hadn’t eaten in two days. He didn’t say much, just held my hand for a moment and whispered “thank you” with tears in his eyes. That moment has stayed with me ever since.

This experience didn’t just make me more aware of the hardships people face — it also made me realize how even small efforts can bring real comfort and dignity to those who feel invisible. It reminded me that in times of crisis, empathy matters as much as action.

Snehalaya NGO – Teaching Children of Sex Workers

At Snehalaya, I worked with children growing up in red-light areas — kids whose lives are shaped by stigma, poverty, and often, silence. I taught them Maths and Science, but what I really hoped to give them was a sense of possibility.

These children were incredibly bright and full of life, despite carrying far more than their share of burdens. Many came to class after helping at home or taking care of younger siblings, but they still showed up eager to learn. One student in particular — a quiet girl with a sharp mind — used to stay back after class to ask questions, not just about school, but about the world beyond her own.

Teaching at Snehalaya was humbling. It showed me that education can be a lifeline — not just for academic success, but for self-worth and hope. These children may come from difficult circumstances, but they deserve every opportunity, and being part of their journey was a privilege I’ll always carry with me.
